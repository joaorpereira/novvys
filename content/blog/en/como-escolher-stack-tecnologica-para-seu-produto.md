Choosing the right tech stack is one of the most critical decisions when starting a digital product. A wrong choice can lead to rework, unnecessary costs, and slow down your company's growth. The right choice, on the other hand, accelerates development, makes hiring talent easier, and ensures the product scales alongside the business.

## Why the Stack Choice Matters So Much

The stack is the foundation of your product. It defines how the software will be built, how the team will collaborate, which integrations will be available, and how much it will cost to maintain the system over time. Unlike decisions that can easily be revisited, migrating stacks at an advanced stage is expensive and risky.

## The 5 Fundamental Criteria

### 1. Fit for the Problem

Before any other consideration, the technology needs to be appropriate for the problem you are solving. A high-concurrency real-time system calls for different approaches than a corporate CRM or an e-learning platform.

Ask yourself: *was this technology designed to solve the type of problem we have?*

### 2. Ecosystem Maturity

Technologies with mature ecosystems offer battle-tested libraries, rich documentation, active communities, and ease of hiring qualified professionals. Adopting an emerging technology can be advantageous for gaining a competitive edge, but it brings risks in support, maintenance, and recruitment.

### 3. Team Capability

The best stack in the world won't compensate if the team doesn't master the tools. Consider the team's current knowledge and the learning curve involved. In projects with tight deadlines, it's preferable to work with familiar technologies and opt for incremental innovations.

### 4. Total Cost of Ownership

Consider not just the development cost, but also:

- Infrastructure costs (cloud, servers)
- License costs (if any)
- Long-term maintenance cost
- Recruitment cost for the chosen technologies

### 5. Scalability and Evolution

Evaluate how the stack performs when the volume of users and data grows. Some technologies scale horizontally with ease; others require more complex architectures to achieve the same level of performance.

## Common Stacks and Their Use Cases

**React + Node.js + PostgreSQL**: Ideal for corporate web applications, SaaS platforms, and products that need high development speed with flexibility to scale.

**Next.js + TypeScript**: Excellent for products requiring SEO, loading performance, and premium user experience.

**Python + FastAPI + PostgreSQL**: Great for systems with a strong data component, integration with machine learning models, and high-throughput APIs.

**React Native / Flutter**: For mobile products that need to run on iOS and Android with a single codebase and native experience.

## The Role of a Software House in the Decision

When you work with an experienced software house, you benefit from technical decisions based on dozens of previous projects. Novvys, for example, analyzes the client's context — team size, product stage, expected user volume, and budget — before any stack recommendation.

This pragmatic perspective is essential to avoid *over-engineering* (using technologies more complex than necessary) or the opposite: choosing tools too simple that limit future growth.

## Conclusion

There is no universally perfect stack. There is the right stack for your context. Make the decision based on objective criteria, listen to those with practical experience, and invest time in technical planning before writing the first line of code. Your future product will thank you.
